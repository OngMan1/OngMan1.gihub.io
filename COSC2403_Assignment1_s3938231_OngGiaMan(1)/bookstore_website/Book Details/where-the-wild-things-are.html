Where The Wild Things Are

210.000

"Where the Wild Things Are" is a children's picture book written and illustrated by Maurice Sendak. It tells the story of a young boy named Max, who after being sent to bed without supper, imagines a magical world inhabited by wild creatures. Max becomes their king and leads them in a wild rumpus, but eventually realizes he wants to return home to his own room and family. The book explores themes of imagination, freedom, and the power of unconditional love. It has become a beloved classic since its publication in 1963, and has been adapted into a successful film and stage production.
